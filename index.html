CIARAN TROTMAN

I am a designer who works between the boundaries of technology and design. I explore how those technologies impact the world around us.

I am currently working as an Immersive Media Designer at Atkins in the UK.

I am constantly looking for the opportunity to work with new people and collaborate on new and interesting projects.

Check out my portfolio to see what my work is like, and please reach out if you're interested in collaborating!

ORA, AN EXPLORATION INTO MIXED REALITY INTERACTION DESIGN

September 2017 - May 2018

This was my thesis for my undergraduate in Product Design and Technology in the University of Limerick. I took the opportunity of the final year design project to explore some design challenges that I found fascinating in the XR sphere, as well as developing my skills in hardware and software rapid prototyping.

PDT GRADUATE BOOKLET

May 2018

I designed the Product Design and Technology Graduate Magazine for the PDT Final Exhibition in 2018 - one hundred perfect bound booklets were printed and on display for the final exhibition, with each student getting a full spread in the magazine

NUDEST ON THE GO

September 2017 - November 2017

An industry-parter project run through the University of Limerick, was recruited with a design team of four to design packaging for the health food company Nudest. The brief was to design a mobile product that would allow users to heat up their breakfast while commuting.

ALIUM, A MODULAR OFFICE REDESIGN

July 2016

This was my entry to a design competition run by Walls to Workstation, Orangebox, and Mola Architects. The brief was to explore how to create a more functional office space, with the theme being the impact of biophilia on the mood and structure of the workspace. I received a high commendation for the project.

DIGITAL VEIL, FUTURE ARTIST MAKERS

July 2016

Digital Veil was a collaborative project run as part of the F.A.M. movement, and was run in conjunction with the Nerve Centre in Derry, and the Ultralab in Madrid. The main focus of the project was to develop an installation piece for an exhibition, focusing on utilising digital fabrication methods. The project was booked for the Liminal exhibition in October, as well as being displayed in Derry and Madrid.
